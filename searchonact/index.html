Design process

To structure the whole design process of the project we are going to present to you today, we have used Design Thinking and in particular, the four phases of the double diamond model, which have allowed us to go from the phase of understanding and defining the problem detected in the field of PRIVACY and CULTURE, to the proposal of a solution that can deliver value and contribute to solving the problem.

Discover

— THE DIGITAL GAP

The digital divide refers to the inequality in access, use, or impact of Information and Communication Technologies (ICT) between social groups. Traditionally considered primarily a question of access, today, with global cell phone penetration at over 95%, relative inequality arises between those with more and less bandwidth and more or less associated skills. Inequality in access to technology is also a form of social exclusion, especially in times of COVID-19. To the extent that we smooth this fissure, we are underpinning a more secure structure for people. Empowering people in vulnerable situations increase their autonomy, promotes equality, and makes us better as a society.

DUALITY between what people say and what they do when they are on these digital platforms sharing their data and accepting everything without knowing what they do with them and acquiring in exchange their navigation > they are willing to pay millions for immaterial things like NFTs or to buy things in the metaverse and then we are faced with real and current data in which there are 2,900 MILLION who DO NOT HAVE access to internet

— Privacy and culture as...

At this point, we have arrived at our definition of the two main themes of the project: "culture" as a connection between people and knowledge sharing and "privacy" as understanding the use and value of personal data on the net. We linked these 2 areas and came up with terms such as: connection, knowledge, diversity, and safe surfing.

— Applied discovery methodologies

In this first phase, we did several interviews that allowed us to get into context, hear directly from people their thoughts and concerns, and test empathy. We also found it very helpful to search the media to be able to understand and adopt the point of view of other main actors in this problem, which we might never have been able to find physically. Finally, a very important tool was the creation of several CJMaps about digital shopping experiences for cultural events, as an example of an online moment where personalization is sought. With this, we realized that in all CJMs a vicious circle is generated in which there are always the same big companies that put forward their own benefits, thus widening the digital divide.

— Target Users and Primary Personas

From the interviews and in general, from all research, we have come to define how are the primary users of our product: Users of the web and/or mobile digital devices with internet access located all over the world, who search the internet daily to consume visual, informative, and cultural content. Continuously connected people, regular internet users who use their mobile or computer to perform daily searches. They usually "accept everything" before accessing a digital service because they know that nowadays "privacy no longer exists". They are aware of the issue of the digital divide and have a moral sense of social responsibility, which they value to be able to bring social benefits through their actions.

— Insight and Challenges

At this point, from everything we discovered in the discovery phase, we draw insights and prioritize them in order to define a How might we scenario and define our main challenges. How could we transform this "legal obligation" to accept terms and conditions into benefits for the community and not only for companies? How could we act within the digital transformation of culture so that everyone can be connected for free and unlimited? So, how do we change privacy? That is, how can we restore its true value to our individual privacy by acting within the digital world? So we start looking for a way that allows us to continue with exactly the same as we normally do in all digital experiences, i.e. using services in exchange for our data and preferences, but using the legal obligation to share valuable data and transforming it into a possibility to generate revenue to concretely solve the problem of the digital divide.

— The Golden Circle - Unique Value Proposition

WHAT do we do? We offer the possibility to generate internet access thanks to your clicks. HOW... through the ads present in our search engine that allow us to generate economic income and thus, thanks to social partners, transform these digital connections into real change and global benefits. WHY Because we want to contribute to reducing the digital divide by putting people first.

We started working on desktop at the beginning of the project but we decided to prioritize mobile for the moment because our archetype "CONNECTED" makes more use of mobile in interviews, they describe it as an "extension of their hand" they use their cell phone more than their laptop also to learn the MOBILE FIRST design mentality

— Business Model Canvas

Once all the design part of our project was developed, we went on to define the business model part, which is central to understand how SearchOnAct would work in reality.

It was also very useful in defining and specifying the actors involved, as well as the the actors involved, as well as the resources required our core values and our Unique Value Proposition the users we are targeting, the type of relationship we want to have with them and the channels we are going to use to do this. And last but not least, the revenue streams, i.e. the sources of income that we have based on a pay-per-click advertising business model in which we generate our revenue through user clicks on promoted ads and we return most of our revenue (70%) to our social partners and use a smaller part for all the resources necessary for the product development.

We also try thanks to an expert in business management and taking as a reference the reports published by Ecosia, one of our main competitors, to define when our project would be a success Business Model: our challenge to be able to understand on a general scale how to unite all the actors involved in generating this change and above all to ideally have a clear and long-term MEASURABLE objective.
